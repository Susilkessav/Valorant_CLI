# Agent Role Responsibilities

## Duelist
**Primary job:** Get the first kill on site entry. Open the site for the team.

- Go in first, but only after initiators have used their utility to clear or soften angles.
- Do not wait for perfect conditions — a slightly imperfect entry with trades is better than never committing.
- On defense: play aggressive off-angles and take early picks. Reset and delay if you lose the first duel, don't gamble again.
- Economy note: Duelists need their guns. If the team is eco and the duelist can full-buy, discuss — a rifle duelist on an eco round can carry.

## Initiator
**Primary job:** Gather info and remove defender advantages before entry.

- Use recon abilities (drones, bolts, sensors) before the team pushes, not after.
- Flashes should enable the entry fragger — throw them toward the angle the entry is peeking, not randomly.
- Concusses/suppresses: use them on known or likely defender positions (common one-ways, default spots).
- On defense: use info abilities to track attackers and alert the team. Save damaging utility for when you know enemies are committed.

## Controller
**Primary job:** Smoke sight lines so the team moves safely and fights on even terms.

- Identify the 2–3 sightlines that block the attack or rotation before the round starts.
- Communicate smoke timings. A smoke nobody knows about doesn't help.
- Do not use all smokes at once — sequence them for the push, save one for post-plant.
- On defense: smoke the attacker entry, not the site itself. Delaying entry is more valuable than blocking off your own angles.

## Sentinel
**Primary job:** Lock down a flank or site to give the team information and prevent getting caught out.

- Place utility (traps, cams, tripwires) before the round starts, not reactively after a push.
- Communicate all triggered alerts immediately — a trap that goes uncalled is wasted.
- On attack: set up behind the team to prevent flanks. Don't enter site unless all flank angles are locked.
- On defense: anchor one site reliably. Being the last player alive on an abandoned site loses rounds.

## Flex / off-role
- If playing a secondary role (e.g., Sentinel player filling Initiator): prioritize the team's core needs over personal comfort abilities.
- Every role is responsible for communicating what they used, what they have left, and what they see.
