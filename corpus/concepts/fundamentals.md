# Core Tactical Fundamentals

## Trading
- Never dry peek alone. Always have a teammate within 2 seconds of trading range.
- The trader positions close enough to refrag, far enough to not die to the same angle.
- On attack, entry fraggers should announce before going in — silence means no trade.
- On defense, after a teammate dies, hold the angle they died to before repositioning.

## Crosshair placement
- Head height at the corner you expect enemies to round.
- Pre-aim common one-way and off-angle spots before reaching them — move, stop, aim, then peek.
- Against Operators: pre-aim wide to avoid the scope's field of view advantage.

## Peeking
- Jiggle peek to gather info without committing to a duel.
- Wide swing when you know the enemy position — it forces them to track a fast target.
- Shoulder peek when you don't know — bait the shot, then punish.
- Never re-peek the same angle after getting a kill without repositioning first.

## Site takes (attack)
1. Gather info with utility (drone, recon bolt, jiggle) before committing anyone.
2. Deploy smokes on the 2–3 spots holding you. Use flashes or concusses to clear, not just damage.
3. Entry fragger commits on a specific call, not randomly.
4. Second player stays close and trades immediately — same angle, different vertical level if possible.
5. Plant for the safest post-plant position, not necessarily default. Spread wide post-plant.

## Site holds (defense)
- One player anchors site; one covers the rotation path. Never leave a site completely empty.
- Utility purpose is to delay the push and gather info — not to get kills. Save kills for when enemies are in the open.
- If 3+ attackers hit one site, it's a retake. The anchor delays; others rotate.
- Rotate only on hard information (two+ footsteps, ability used, confirmed call). One footstep is a bait.

## Mid-round adjustments
- If attackers haven't committed to a site by 60 seconds, expect a rush on the weakest side.
- Defenders with a numbers advantage mid-round should play for picks, not an early retake.
- Attackers with utility still unused should not default to a dry rush — use it.
