# Retake Principles

## When to retake vs. reset
- Retake if: you have 3+ alive, spike is planted, at least 40 seconds remain.
- Reset (don't retake) if: you are down 2+ players, or only 1 defender remains against 3+.
- A bad retake wastes both player lives and credits. It's better to win the half with economy intact.

## Retake execution
1. **Delay first.** Smokes, mollies, and flashes buy time for teammates to arrive. The anchor should not challenge alone.
2. **Come from multiple angles.** A retake from one entry is the easiest to hold. Split — at least two different directions.
3. **Identify the spike location before committing.** Entry from a bad side gives attackers free kills on the other.
4. **Flashbang before entering site.** Initiators should use flashes or concusses so the fraggers enter with an advantage, not a duel.
5. **Defuse under cover.** Use a smoke, a Sage wall, or a Killjoy ult. Never defuse in the open unless the site is 100% clear.

## Clutch retake (1vX)
- Stall the defuse by any means: flanking, grenades, making noise. Buy time for the spike to become a 1v1 window.
- Never rush the spike directly — attackers are waiting. Clear angles one at a time from safe positions.
- Use the spike beep audio to track its defuse progress without approaching.
- If 1v2+: play for picks. Get one kill to even the odds, then reassess. Rushing the spike with enemies alive is almost always a loss.

## Post-plant defense (attacking side)
- After planting, spread across multiple angles so defenders can't clear you as a stack.
- Dedicate one player to watching the defuse — this player should not be pressured to frag.
- Use mollies and flashes on defender entry routes, not on the spike (unless stopping a defuse).
- If the defender forces defuse under pressure: trade kills, don't abandon the spike to chase.

## Common retake mistakes
- Rotating too late (waiting for visual confirmation instead of trusting audio/calls).
- Stacking the same entry as the anchor — all angles still covered for defenders.
- Defusing before clearing all corners — entry kill after defuse start is the most common retake loss.
