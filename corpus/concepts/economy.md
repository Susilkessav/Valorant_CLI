# Valorant Economy Rules

## Round income
- Win: 3000 credits
- Loss (1st): 1900 credits
- Loss (2nd consecutive): 2400 credits
- Loss (3rd+ consecutive): 2900 credits
- Plant spike (regardless of win/loss): +300 to planter, +200 to all teammates

## Buy thresholds
- Full buy: 3900+ (Vandal/Phantom + full shields + abilities)
- Half buy / force: 2000–3900 (Spectre/Marshal + light shields + key abilities)
- Eco / save: under 2000 (Classic/Sheriff only, save credits for next round)

## Pistol round
- Attackers: Classic or Ghost + 1 ability. Sheriff if you prefer range.
- Defenders: Ghost or Sheriff. Save remaining credits for first-buy round.
- Win pistol → buy Spectre/Marshal + light shields round 2. Don't full-buy yet.
- Lose pistol → full save round 2 unless the team has 3000+ each from a plant bonus.

## Loss bonus tracking
- After winning a round following a loss streak, opponents get 1900 (reset). They may force.
- Track enemy consecutive losses to predict their economy. Two losses → they have ~4800 total over 2 rounds, likely to force.

## Team economy discipline
- If 3 or more teammates can't afford a rifle + light shields, everyone saves together (full eco).
- One player full-buying while others save wastes the round without meaningful firepower.
- Rifle + 0 shields beats Spectre + full shields in most duels — buy the gun first.

## Key principles
- Plant the spike every eco round. The +300/+200 often converts an eco into a half-buy.
- Shield priority: always buy light shields (400) over heavy (1000) when credits are tight.
- Ability priority: signature/free ability always active; buy 1 purchased ability minimum on half-buys.
